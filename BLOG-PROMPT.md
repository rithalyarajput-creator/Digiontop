## What you must produce

Deliver ALL of the following, in this order, clearly labelled:

### 1. Keyword research (do this FIRST, before writing)

State plainly:
- **Primary keyword** — the exact phrase an Indian business owner would type into Google. Long-tail (3–6 words), buying intent. Not a vanity term like "digital marketing".
- **5–8 secondary keywords** — related phrases to work into H2s and body text naturally.
- **Search intent** — is the reader trying to *learn*, *compare*, or *buy*? Say which, and write accordingly.
- **Why this keyword is winnable** — one honest sentence. If the topic I gave you is far too competitive to rank for (e.g. "digital marketing"), say so and propose a narrower angle instead. Do not pretend a hopeless keyword is winnable.

### 2. SEO metadata

- **Meta title** — ≤60 characters, primary keyword near the front. Do NOT append "| DigionTop" (the site adds it).
- **Meta description** — 150–160 characters. Include the primary keyword. End with a reason to click.
- **URL slug** — lowercase, hyphenated, contains the primary keyword, no stop-words. e.g. `shopify-store-setup-cost-india`
- **Excerpt** — 2 sentences, ~40 words, for the blog listing card.
- **Tags** — 4–6 comma-separated tags.

### 3. The article

**Length:** 1,500–2,200 words. Shorter than 1,200 will not rank.

**Structure:**
- **H1** — the article title. Contains the primary keyword. Compelling, not clickbait.
- **Opening (100–150 words)** — Do NOT open with "In today's digital world…". Open with the reader's actual problem, a surprising number, or a direct question. The primary keyword must appear naturally in the first 100 words.
- **H2 sections** — 5–8 of them, each covering one clear sub-topic. Work secondary keywords into these headings naturally — never force them.
- **H3s** where a section needs sub-points.
- **Bullet lists and numbered steps** — Indian readers skim on mobile. Break up every long block.
- **A comparison table** where it genuinely helps (pricing, platform vs platform, before/after).
- **Real numbers** — costs in ₹, timelines in weeks, percentages. If you don't know a real figure, give an honest range and say it's a range. **Never invent a statistic or cite a study that doesn't exist.**
- **Internal links** — link to 3–5 of our service pages above, using the exact URLs, where they are genuinely relevant to the sentence. Do not stuff them in.
- **FAQ section (H2)** — 5–6 real questions people ask, each with a 40–80 word answer. This is what wins Google's FAQ rich results.
- **Conclusion + CTA (H2)** — Summarise in 3 lines, then one clear call to action: either the free SEO audit (https://www.digiontop.com/services/seo-audit) or contact (https://www.digiontop.com/contact). Pick whichever fits the article.

**Formatting:** Return the article as clean HTML using only these tags: `<h1> <h2> <h3> <p> <strong> <em> <ul> <ol> <li> <a href> <blockquote> <table> <tr> <th> <td>`. No inline styles, no classes, no `<div>`s. This pastes straight into our blog editor.

Featured image must be **1200 × 630 px (16:9)**.

### 5. Distribution copy

Once the article is written, also give me:

- **Google Business Profile post** (~250 words) — hook in the first line, ✅ bullet points, keywords worked in, ends with "Read the full guide →". This goes on our GMB with a "Learn more" button linking to the article.
- **LinkedIn post** (~150 words) — written for a founder's personal profile, not a company page. Conversational, one insight, ends with a question to drive comments.

## Rules — these are not optional

1. **Never fabricate.** No made-up statistics, no invented case studies, no fake client names, no "according to a recent study" unless you can name the real study. An honest range beats a confident lie.
2. **Write for the reader, not the algorithm.** Keyword density is not a target. If a sentence reads badly with the keyword in it, leave the keyword out.
3. **India-specific.** ₹ pricing, Indian examples, Indian platforms (UPI, Flipkart, Meesho, Razorpay), Indian business realities.
4. **No AI tells.** Avoid: "delve", "in today's fast-paced world", "unlock the power of", "elevate your", "game-changer", "moreover", "furthermore", "it is important to note that". Vary sentence length. Write like a person.
5. **Be genuinely useful.** The reader should be able to act on this article even if they never hire us. That's what earns the trust that converts.
6. If I give you a topic that's a bad idea (too broad, no search demand, or something we can't credibly write about), **say so and propose a better angle** rather than writing it anyway.

# How to use this

1. Copy the whole prompt above
2. Paste into Claude
3. At the bottom, replace `[← TYPE YOUR TOPIC HERE]` with your topic, e.g.:
   - `How much does a website cost in India in 2026?`
   - `Shopify vs WooCommerce for Indian businesses`
   - `Why my Google Ads aren't converting`
4. Claude gives you: keywords → meta → full article HTML → image briefs → GMB + LinkedIn posts
5. Paste the HTML into your blog editor (Visual tab handles it — bold, links, headings all carry over)
6. Fill in meta title / description / slug / excerpt / tags from what it gave you
7. Create the images in Canva using its briefs, name the files exactly as it says
8. Publish → then post the GMB copy on your Business Profile → then LinkedIn

# Topic formulas that rank

- **How to** → `How to Set Up a Shopify Store in India (Step-by-Step)`
- **Vs** → `Shopify vs WooCommerce: Which Is Better for Indian Businesses?`
- **Cost** → `How Much Does a Website Cost in India in 2026?` ← highest buying intent
- **Mistakes** → `7 SEO Mistakes Killing Your Website Traffic`
- **Best/Top** → `10 Best Shopify Apps for Indian Stores`
